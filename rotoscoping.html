Project Page

Rotoscoping

This rotoscoping project is based on the music video for Hate Me by Ellie Goulding and Juice WRLD, using traced motion and stylized frames to reinterpret the song's emotional and visual atmosphere.

Overview

Transforming a music video into stylized motion.

This project studies gesture, timing, silhouette, and mood. By carefully tracing selected frames from the official music video, the final animation keeps the original performance energy while introducing a darker and more authored visual style.

Visual Direction

How the song shapes the look

  • Use scenes from the music video with clear gestures, dramatic lighting, and emotional impact.
  • Break the sequence into key poses and motion transitions for tracing.
  • Emphasize contrast, silhouette, and expressive outlines over minor detail.
  • Refine each frame so the sequence feels smooth, moody, and visually cohesive.

Creative Focus

What defines the rotoscope treatment

Mood

Dark, intimate, and emotionally tense

The design direction reflects the song's heartbreak, conflict, and emotional push-and-pull.

Motion

Grounded in real performance

The traced movement keeps the original gestures believable while making the visuals more stylized.

Style

Strong outlines and selective detail

The final result focuses attention on expression, shape, and the emotional rhythm of the song.
